A fight

It is almost impressive how, in moments where distress accumulates, everything can explode. When I think about this fight we had in Medell√≠n, I do not think about what happened on the plane, but about what came after, about the sequence of events that spread from a combination of misunderstandings and lack of communication.

I remember the way to the Uber, my shock at what had just happened, and the small moments that led to what unfolded in the apartment. I still think the same about what I am sure of: nothing could justify touching the child of a stranger. That silence of mine, which carries so much meaning for you, you saw it as a punishment. It was rather the weight of processing everything that had happened. In the Uber we were sitting there, but oblivious to each other. Every minute we spent in silence we were hurting ourselves.

For me, looking at our relationship in retrospect and at how I see the world, it makes me think that at some point in our lives we will see our partner do things that clash with what we believed and with everything that represents that divinity that is our bond. Love has different facets: pain, fear, and in this case one of them is when we stop looking for that idealized image of us and start negotiating with the reality of the other person.

This for me has been a real conflict, a real conflict between two people. One of those where it is not enough to just hide or set things aside, but rather one that calls us to a deeper inner reality and to question whether we are truly reciprocated. Today I think that these conflicts are not destructive. They help us to clarify, almost like a catharsis from which both people emerge with more knowledge and much more strength, and in that sense, with much more tenderness.

I was furious when we arrived at the Airbnb. Thirty thousand things went through my head, and I am sure through yours as well. I have always told you that for me many times you are like a scared little bunny, a delicate soul that in an attempt at self preservation flees. You told me you wanted to leave, that you did not want to spend the night with me. And I thought you were being silly, to be honest. It is just a matter of seeing the love I have for you. Of course I am furious, of course I do not feel like talking, but for God‚Äôs sake, I love you too much. From the second you told me you felt abandoned that time I left your apartment in Melbourne, I have never thought about doing it again, much less in Medell√≠n.

You asked me for the wifi password like someone asking for an emergency exit, convinced that for me you had already become a villain with no return. But what you did not see under my layer of anger is that for me there are no villains between us, only tired humans. I refused to give you that password not out of pride, but because my love is infinitely more rational than my rage. I could not let you go adrift in a strange city just because our words had become tangled. My protection toward you does not turn off when we yell at each other. On the contrary, that is when it becomes most alert, because you are the most precious thing I have to take care of in this world.

When I finally sat on that bed and told you that you mattered to me, I felt how the air came back into the room. By hugging you and feeling how your body relaxed against mine, I understood that I was not hugging a villain, but the love of my life. I judged you too much that time. In the hunger of my morality and ethics I did not find your voice. With the contact of your body I remembered the value of loving you.

It is the noise that sometimes deceives us into thinking that love has been extinguished. But remember, beautiful, that I am a man who loves your eyes, and even more, our gazes. If you ever notice that I look into your eyes and you recognize a streak of love in mine, do not think it is a delusion. Simply think that you can count on me.

Because real love is that pact where, even if we have hurt each other with silences in a car or with words in a hallway, we decide that the refuge is still each other. Thank you for not leaving, for staying to fight for us, and for letting me be the place where your scared little bunny can always find peace.

The Muse
I think about how magical this place was. We both know well that this library is the pride of every architect in Colombia. It has a power and a unique simplicity that I still think about from time to time. And then you arrived in Bogota at the Virgilio Barco. The center of gravity shifted completely. Those walls that have heard our names seemed to lean toward you for a moment. The windows were brighter as they watched you pass and the beautiful ochre colors of the library seemed more vivid than ever. You are like an inevitable force in my life and you do not adapt to the place. It was the place that reorganized itself around you so that its famous beauty could be the perfect frame for your face. The Virgilio Barco was waiting for you to arrive so that perspective would finally have a reason to exist.

The Night Out

We had dinner at Selma, still carrying the bitterness of the afternoon, for reasons only life understands. Forgive me for my silences and my outbursts. I think about our arguments throughout the years, about that tension that lingers in the air, waiting for a simple misunderstanding to break it. If you think about these last few days, there is nothing more intimate than a disagreement. In those moments of anger, we reveal ourselves to each other with a nakedness that even sex can never reach.

I remember it started with me, my insecurities and my fears. You tried to comfort me, yet, look at me, I am as stubborn as a mule. Stubborn as I am, I fight with you because I need you to see me, not as you want me to be, but as I truly am. To fight with you is my way of telling you that you are the only person in the world who holds the power to make me suffer.

Luckily, our arguments never last long, and somehow, we always find the path back to one another.

YOUR BIRTHDAY (AUG 3)
Rumba at Andrés with Family

That morning of your birthday still haunts me because I felt like I didn't put in the effort you deserved. I remember your hints and your desire to do something special but I stayed quiet and did nothing to make you feel like the princess you are. I limited myself to walking through Bogota as if we were fulfilling a task, I mean I couldn't feel it but I got lost in the logistics and the money, neglecting the sacred detail of making you feel that your existence is the only monument worth admiring.

Now when you mention that day I feel a crack opening in the ground we walk on because I know you are right to feel like my love had no weight. It hurts me to have left that mark of loneliness in your memory when you are the root that has made my desert bloom and the only homeland I want to belong to. Today I would give anything to go back and prepare that breakfast for you, to look into your eyes so you would have no doubt that you are everything I need. You are the most incredible woman I know and you deserve for every day to be a tribute to your existence, not just because I give it to you but because celebrating you comes from my soul.

A Note on Perspectives

My Love,

Hello beautiful.

I do not know how to start this. Those days were like a sea of mixed emotions. Do not misunderstand me because there are beautiful things that happened and that I treasure, but there is a certain weight in me, a shadow that grew from what happened on your birthday.

I am a man with an inner dryness that often makes me feel ashamed. I have ignored so many things that other people consider natural, perhaps because of my upbringing or my own way of growing up and being in this world with that unbearable lightness that you often point out with such determination. I have become used to being this way, thinking that love is a safe harbor, something that is built once and left there like a house. Many times I feel that we are there in that refuge, but we live under the same roof without truly being in the same room. It was arrogant of me to take for granted that everything would be okay or to think that you would understand me and like what I did just because I put effort into it. In the end I was not a wicked man, but I was a distracted man, and distraction in love is a silent way of ruining things. I planned so much for the trip to go well and I was so obsessed with making you proud of my country that I got lost in the itinerary when the only thing I had to do was stop looking into the map and look into your eyes.

I always tell you that our fights lead us to something better and today I understand more than ever that we have built this beautiful house together, but we have to know how to live in it. We are growing every day and sometimes we make mistakes, but I do not want this to be a dead end. I want it to be the start of something better. After every battle I want to sit with you and learn to translate your silences. I know I am a man of deep habits, but for you I am willing to learn a new language. I do not want us to be in different rooms under the same roof. I want us to build that better world where my effort and your sensitivity finally speak the same language. If you were able to make my roots bloom in this cold soil, I am sure we can find our own way of saying I am here without any maps between us.

After all, while I was editing that short photo session in that bookstore in Colombia, I saw your smile. It is that same smile I see in every photo I take of you, so unique and recognizable when our gazes meet. I realized that my best photograph will always be the one that manages to capture that light in your eyes when you recognize me, that moment where you stop being for the rest of the world and we simply find each other. That's what I want forever.

With a lot of love, Andrés.

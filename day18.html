I look at you and realize that you are my center of gravity. If someone asked me what I admire, I would say it is your ability to be in the world with a burning purity, with such pure and strong love. Sometimes I think that I don't just love you, but also something that you have given me. You are the perfect interpretation of my moments, of my personality. When I write, when I talk to you, I feel understood. It is to you that I try to explain the world because only through your eyes the world seems to have real weight and not an unbearable lightness.

Without you, my words, my being, the world feels empty, because you are the one who gives them the color and pain that makes them real.

This is the star that represents the heart of the constellation; it is the central point. For me, that star is you. You are for me the kind of perfection that God has placed on earth to remind us that heaven exists. My soul is so full of you that there is no room for anything but your image. Along with this page, I present the photo that I look at every day. Every time I want to check my messages, there you are, with that beautiful smile surrounded by all your stuffed animals. After all, you are the star that guides my heart.

MuThe future and the danger

Sometimes I feel that I am still a beginner in this, Eunice. I realize that we have to learn to love again every day, and I want to do it with all my strength, with my whole being gathered in this heart of mine that sometimes feels lonely, distressed and beating too loudly. It scares me to think that the future could become a threat, simply because this long distance love is at the mercy of time and of that freedom of yours which, although I admire it, reminds me that you could step away at any moment. The deepest terror is not what we plan, but what happens to us while we look the other way, that lack of control over what will come after our last kiss before the airport separates us again.

Every time I have to leave you and return to Madrid, I feel that I carry a piece of you with me, but I am also invaded by doubt about what will happen while I am away. I wonder what you might have thought in the silence of Australia or whether, because of the kilometers, you might have forgotten our very unique way of looking at things. My real fear is not that you leave physically, but that time and routine turn us into two strangers who greet each other politely on a street they no longer recognize, losing that complicity we have built between letters and screens.

However, in the midst of that uncertainty, I choose to believe in what we are building. I know I am not perfect and that I carry much to amend, but it is you, with your infinite patience and that light you emanate even on my darkest days, who makes me want to be better. You are my refuge and my greatest certainty, and that is why I am here, willing to keep working on us right now, so that this bond does not become a distant echo, but a work that heals and grows deeper with every wound overcome. Acknowledging our shadows is the purest way I have to tell you that I trust you, that I admire you deeply and that I choose, one and a thousand times, for this distance to force us to love each other with a delicacy we did not know before. This star is the cabella, it is the one that thinks about the future and approaches the fears.

GenubiMy eyes

Well, baby. The star itself tells you what it means, it's exactly that: the eyes of the constellation. In this case, I want to get closer to my favorite photo of you.

In these pages, I've told you what I think about photography, and you know I'm always very pretentious about it. But the existence of this photo represents my greatest truth; that “this has been” that guarantees me that your body was there, in front of my lens, just as it was in front of my eyes. It's not a dead image; it's the fragment that marks my memory, a before and after. When I took this photo, I wasn't just trying to capture your beauty, but our intimacy, which at that moment was growing little by little, gaining ground in the silence.

It was the first portrait I took of you. I'm pretty sure you don't let just anyone take your picture, and you know I don't like to photograph just anyone; yet there I was, trying to find you through the viewfinder. I don't photograph for others, but for ourselves. I wanted proof that we had been there, that that light and that skin existed in an unrepeatable conjunction, just at the exact moment when we were beginning to be ourselves, finally losing our fear of our own relationship. I caught you, sneaky sneaky, smiling at me as you always do, but at that time it was a smile that was still unknown to me.

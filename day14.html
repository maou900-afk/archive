The First flavors of Malaysia

I can't lie, and it won't come as a surprise to you, but the first thing that comes out of my mouth when I talk about Penang is the food. What a wealth of flavors, of unique experiences. In the end, the body isn't meant to be saved, it's meant to be enjoyed, especially if it's by your side. I want to literally eat the world, try everything that comes our way, and in the end, I can always blame you for making me fat. However, this is only the first moment of what we ate in Malaysia.

When I think about that first period, I think about your father, arriving with the smell of durian from a block away. You always told me about your father's kindness, but when he arrived with $3,000 worth of durian, it was the physical demonstration of your words. I said to myself, yep, it won't be that bad, the pain and disgust is mental. I thought, there must be someone in this household who doesn't like durian. I was quite wrong. Everyone began to eat as if it were the food of the heavens. Love, time for bed, I said. But how naive of me to think that this was over. That day was probably the day I ate the most durian in my life. I asked for rice as a cry for help. The first few bites were quite normal. After my hundred and first bite, I wanted to surrender. No joke, I'd rather die than look bad in front of your parents. The heat at the dinner table was making me dizzy, the smell and the taste. Yet there you were with that beautiful smile of yours, spending time with your family. And I knew that durian was worth it.

I ate. A lot. But there is one king among kings.

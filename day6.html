How did we arrive here?

Upon returning to Melbourne, I not only conversed with the city that had given me shelter, but also with myself. Despite having been away for only a short time, I felt like a stranger; I understood that one does not settle in a country, but rather in a time, and mine there was gone. In the midst of that strangeness, I realised that I had not returned out of nostalgia for Melbourne, but out of the certainty of having you.

Amidst all the chaos of recent months, and the distance that those months in Switzerland represented, we wanted to get closer to normality, so we cooked together. Oh boy...

Working

Was I in love? Yes, I am and always have been. I aspire to be the man capable of giving you the world I once promised. Everything I do, I do it thinking that you will like it, that you will be proud of me. I am always in competition with myself. So I started working for Uber Eats to be able to give you something better while I was there.

I fell off my bike. I ripped my pants completely in the middle of the street and I still carry the scar on my knee. It was painful and frustrating, if I close my eyes I can still live that moment vividly. I remember that as soon as I got up after the accident that the only thing that came to mind was that I wouldn't be able to treat you to a nice dinner because of me. I stood there, contemplating my life, my knee was burning like hell. It’s strange how in a moment of crisis, your mind strips everything away until only one single line of thought remains and for me, that was you. I realized then that I would gladly trade a thousand falls. why? Because all those scars would simply be the price I’d pay just to see you smile across a dinner table.

The Closing Note

Goodnight, Hermosa, Welcome to day 6. Short letter today Sometimes, I mix this trip to my life in Melbourne, and for a moment, I realize that time felt like a glimpse of the best life we could have. It was the closest we ever got to a daily life together in Australia, and I know I will miss those moments for the rest of my life. But I must confess our relationship has grown so much since then that our Melbourne trip is just one chapter in our incredible collection of memories. You have made every single one of those moments unforgettable. Those old past days didn’t stay frozen in time, of course. Those days when we lived just 20 minutes apart moved forward with us, they transformed and became the solid ground we walk on today. Because of that, I don’t love you the way I did in Australia, I love you more and more improtantly, better. it's because I love you with that certainty of someone who knows no matter where we are, you are my destination. Long time ago I stopped to belong to a specific location, my favorite place in the world isn’t a city or a memory anymore. It’s you.
Andrés.

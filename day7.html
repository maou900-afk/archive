The gap

When our distance relationship started to shape
When I arrived in Colombia from Australia, I arrived as a new person. With a different view of the world and a new relationship in my suitcase. You have always been the only person I can talk to about the shadow of a cloud, and even though you may not believe me, about mundane and meaningless things. I filled Colombia with memories of you, and when something was new, I discovered it with you.

We were so far apart, probably further apart geographically than we will ever be. But there is something beautiful about this pain; we are not together out of habit or geographical convenience, but because of a choice we renew every time we decide to call each other every day. The ocean that separates us is not a wall, it is a space we are filling with our determination not to forget each other. At this point, my mind is so full of you that sometimes I stumble over my own thoughts about us as if they were pieces of furniture in a dark room.

Our persistence is a quiet rebellion against our own shadows.

A LETTER FROM THE FUTURE SPAIN

Hello, hermosa,

Today I don't want to write about this part of our lives, when I arrived to Colombia. I wanted to reach about what happened today for me yestarday for you. Big fight.

I must confess that in the midst of the noise of life it seems I have lost sight of the support I promised you. It is complex I know we carry so many things that it is too easy to forget what is essential. This morning after our fight I saw you talk about your past crying inconsolably letting out your frustration with me and fighting against yourself. I stayed there watching you and I remembered something I have always believed that couples are not two perfect people who are disappointed by each others flaws but two wounded people trying to help each other heal. I had that philosophy clear but I had forgotten my role which is to be your benevolent interpreter. Love in the end consists of navigating each others neuroses with patience recognizing that we are both on some level survivors of our own stories.

In my rage and my desire to be heard I judged you more than I should have. I know I had my reasons and that boundaries were crossed but what truly weighs on me is that love only heals when it allows us to show our most broken parts without the fear of being abandoned and for a long time I have not made you feel that security. For a moment I stopped recognizing your story.

I know you still carry your ghosts but I want to affirm to you today that there is nothing in your past no matter how dark it may seem to you that scares me enough to let you go. Even today when rage took me to the limit of thinking it my final choice is still you. You are the most valuable project I have ever decided to participate in. I deeply admire the courage with which you face your shadows even when you feel they are defeating you.

I want to invite you to walk our maps in a different way together. In the heat of arguments our old defenses rise up and we look at each other as enemies. I would like us to try little by little to make this space so safe that you no longer need those weapons to protect yourself from me because I am on your team.

Without me and especially with me you are worthy of being loved. I love you bravely with your wounds and I want to be a witness to your reconstruction. I hope that one day you see me and that above the fear you feel that my presence is stronger than your ghosts. Because at the end of the day my only desire is to be the place where your nervous system finds peace and where your heart knows without any doubt that it no longer has to fight anymore.

With love, Andrés.
